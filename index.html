B2Church UX Checklist

Know

Is it clear what our goals are? Schedule a meeting or email the PM/PO of the task.

Do we know who our users are and where they are coming from?

  • DR.com Proto Personas
  • FPU Funnel Personas (PDF)
  • FPU Product Personas (PDF)

Are there any sacred cows surrounding what we are doing? Will they provide a blocker?

Research

Find out the woes of our users from these fine folks on the front lines. Sit with Tech Support and share notes (Evernote) with the team.

Can we identify the problem from data analytics? Check the following in Adobe Analytics and share with PO/PM.

  1. Conversion Rate
  2. Bounce Rate
  3. Exit Rate
  4. Page Flows

Do we have any survey data or research to help us know what our users have been telling us? We use SurveyGizmo and Tony is the keymaster. We also have data from past surveys.

  • Send Questions to Tony Lemonis
Plan

From user stories, flows, goals, get with a small team to brainstorm solutions. Will the solutions be useful to our users?

  • Organize the ideas for clarity and share with the team in Slack.

Once brainstorm is done, analytic results are compiled, survey research found, and tech support feedback is in, organize the information in a way that makes sense and will provide clarity on what to do next.

  • Share info with the team via Evernote FPU Funnel.

Use all the information to create a user flow. Is it clear what our users will do within our product? What are all the paths a user could take? Are there any dead ends?

  • Lucidchart (Quick User Flows)
  • User Flow Templates
Explore

Add some details and structure to our ideas, reuse existing patterns and create pages on top of our user flows. Is it clear what each page should do?

  • Use Balsamiq Mockups or Sketch to create a wireframe.

Test early sketches with our users on Invision using the sketches/wireframes. This will provide enough feedback to either go back to refine sketches and/or user flows.

  • Set up a test in usertesting.com and use the Invision link you created as the Prototype URL.

Once sketches/wireframes are reworked and ready for hi-fidedlity mockups, document requirements in JIRA for team clarity on how the interactions should go and provide examples for both designers and developers.

  • Atlassian Cloud (JIRA)
Create

Work knee-to-knee with the web designer to come up with an incredible user interface that adheres to usability and creative principles such as efficiency and familiarity.

Is it trustworthy, on brand, and empathetic? Get with content writers and marketing to get some preliminary content in place. All content must go through copyediting before being pushed to production.

Once content and mockups are somewhat created and make sense, go through and make sure that everything is digestible, visible, responsive, and flexible.

Test

Once product is pushed to production, unmoderated user testing helps us gauge if users are having friction in any areas of our product.

  • Set up a test in usertesting.com.
  • User Testing Reference

Helps us test small ideas over a smaller amount of time before investing too much time in building something that doesn't work.

  • NOTE: This type of testing must not happen when usability tests are in action.
  • Optimizely (A/B Tests)

Data analytics will give quick insight to conversion, bounce, exit, and page flows.

  • NOTE: These results may be skewed if running at same time as A/B tests.
  • Adobe Analytics
Delight

Is it understandable? Go through label text and make sure it is readable and that it will make sense to any user in any situation.

  • Ramsey UX Microcopy

Are there any triggers, rules, feedback, and loops? Look for little interactions that are along the user path and how each of these may change the path, animate, provide messaging, etc.

Go through interface and determine is there any place for animation that would make certain interactions delightful.

  • Look for examples or create one in CodePen and discuss with designers and developers.
